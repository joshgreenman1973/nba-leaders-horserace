Methodology & sources

Every bar in The Climb is a real, sourced number. This page documents exactly where the data comes from, how the running totals are built, and what the known limitations are. Nothing is a black box.

What the charts show

Four synchronized races track the NBA’s all-time regular-season leaders in four counting stats. As the shared clock advances one season at a time, each bar is the player’s cumulative career total through the end of that season. Retired players keep their final total and hold their place on the board until someone climbs past them.

  • PTS Points — from the 1946–47 season
  • REB Total rebounds — from 1950–51
  • AST Assists — from 1946–47
  • STL Steals — from 1973–74

Source

All figures are the official season totals published by Basketball-Reference.com, pulled from each season’s totals page (e.g. /leagues/NBA_2024_totals.html, and /leagues/BAA_1947_totals.html for the league’s first three seasons under its original name, the Basketball Association of America). One season’s page lists every player’s totals for that year; summing them in chronological order yields each player’s running career total.

How a running total is built

  • For each season from 1946–47 to 2024–25, every player’s single-season total for the stat is read from that season’s page.
  • Players are identified by their unique Basketball-Reference player ID (e.g. jamesle01), so totals accumulate correctly even for players with identical names or accented spellings.
  • A player traded mid-season appears on Basketball-Reference as one row per team plus a combined-total row (labeled 2TM, 3TM, or TOT). We keep only the combined row, so each season is counted exactly once.
  • Totals carry forward through seasons a player did not play, so retired legends stay on the board and get chased down rather than disappearing.

Limitations & honest caveats

  • Regular season only. Playoff totals are not included.
  • NBA/BAA only. Statistics a player accumulated in the rival ABA (1967–1976) are not part of these NBA career totals — this affects players like Julius Erving and Moses Malone.
  • Stats began when the league began tracking them. Rebounds were not recorded before 1950–51 and steals not before 1973–74. Those early years are intentionally left blank, never zero-filled, and each chart only starts racing once its stat exists. Blocks and turnovers (also first tracked in 1973–74) are not shown here.
  • Recency. Totals run through the completed 2024–25 season. Current standings will shift as active players keep accumulating.
  • Always spot-check any single figure against Basketball-Reference before citing it.

Build

Data was scraped once into data/raw_seasons.json (25,171 player-seasons), then reduced to cumulative keyframes in data/data.json by a transparent script (build.mjs). The page is static HTML/CSS/JavaScript with no tracking and no external dependencies beyond the web fonts. Built with AI assistance; the data pipeline is open and reproducible.
